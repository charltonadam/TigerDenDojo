Your Instructor

Sensei Brandon Chambers

Founder & Head Instructor — Tiger Den Dojo

Sensei Chambers is the founder of Tiger Den Dojo and the creator of Shoukendou Karate, a dynamic martial arts system he developed through years of dedicated study and teaching. His system blends the best elements of Kempo, Shotokan Karate, Taekwondo, and Kobudo (weapons) into a comprehensive, practical approach to self-defense and personal development.

What sets Sensei Chambers apart is his genuine investment in each student's journey. Whether you're a 3-year-old taking your first class or an adult rediscovering the martial arts, he adapts his instruction to meet you where you are.

Students and parents consistently describe him as patient, precise, and deeply passionate about the art — someone who pushes you to grow while creating an environment where no one feels intimidated. He has worked with students of diverse backgrounds and needs, including those with autism, demonstrating a commitment to inclusive and accessible martial arts training.

The Art

Shoukendou Karate

The name Shoukendou (pronounced "show-ken-doh") translates from Japanese as "The Way of the Honorable Fist." It is a martial arts system created by Sensei Brandon Chambers to be practical, well-rounded, and grounded in traditional values.

Rather than limiting students to a single style, Shoukendou draws from four traditions:

Kempo

Fluid, multi-directional strikes and practical self-defense applications.

Shotokan Karate

Strong, linear technique, powerful stances, and classical kata.

Taekwondo

Dynamic kicking techniques and speed-based footwork.

Kobudo (Weapons)

Traditional Okinawan weapons training for advanced students.

Our Values

The Pillars of Training

Every class at Tiger Den Dojo is built on four core values. These are not just words posted on a wall — they are actively taught, discussed, and expected in practice.

Respect

For your instructor, your training partners, your opponents, and yourself. Respect is practiced in every bow.

Honor

Acting with integrity in all situations — not just on the mat. How you train reflects how you live.

Gratitude

Appreciation for the opportunity to learn, for those who teach you, and for the body that allows you to train.

Pride

Taking genuine pride in your progress, your effort, and representing Tiger Den Dojo with excellence.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Shoukendou Karate? +

Shoukendou Karate is a martial arts system created by Sensei Brandon Chambers that blends techniques from Kempo, Shotokan Karate, Taekwondo, and Kobudo. The name translates as "The Way of the Honorable Fist" and reflects both the physical training and the philosophical values — respect, honor, gratitude, and pride — that guide every class.

What age can my child start? +

We accept students as young as 3 years old in our Tiny Tigers program. These classes are specifically designed for young children with age-appropriate activities focused on fun, following directions, and basic movement. There's no minimum martial arts experience required.

Do I need any prior experience to join? +

Absolutely not. Every program at Tiger Den Dojo is designed to welcome complete beginners. Sensei Chambers adapts instruction to your starting point, and you'll receive two free trial classes to experience the training before making any commitment.

What should I wear to my first class? +

For your two free trial classes, comfortable athletic clothing (t-shirt, gym pants or shorts) is all you need. No gear required. Once you formally enroll:

  • Tiny Tigers wear a dojo t-shirt (available through us)
  • All other students will need a traditional gi (uniform) and sparring gear set, available for approximately $200–$300+ through the dojo
Are there annual contracts? +

No — Tiger Den Dojo does not require annual contracts. Tuition is month-to-month. We want students here because they love training and see real value in it, not because they're locked into a commitment they regret.

How long does it take to earn my first belt? +

Belt promotions are based entirely on demonstrated skill, not on a fixed time schedule. When Sensei Chambers determines you're ready, he'll invite you to test. Students who train consistently and apply themselves can typically earn their first belt within a few months, but there's no rush — the journey matters more than the destination.

Can my child with special needs participate? +

Yes! Sensei Chambers has experience teaching students with a variety of needs, including students with autism. He takes a patient, individualized approach and is committed to making martial arts accessible and beneficial for everyone. We encourage you to contact us directly to discuss your child's specific needs so we can set them up for success.

Do I have to participate in tournaments? +

Tournaments are completely optional. For students who want the experience, there are two in-school tournaments per year as well as external tournaments available for students ages 6 and up. External tournaments typically cost $40–$150+ to enter. Many students find them to be an exciting way to test their skills in a supportive environment, but no one is ever required to compete.

What is the Black Belt Club? +

At the Red Belt level, students are invited to join the Black Belt Club with a one-time $50 membership fee. This is a symbolic commitment to pursuing the black belt and represents a serious dedication to the martial arts journey. It is required to continue training beyond the Red Belt level.

Can I drop in without scheduling first? +

Drop-ins are not accepted at Tiger Den Dojo. All students — including new trial students — must schedule their class in advance. This allows Sensei Chambers to prepare appropriately and ensure every student gets the attention they deserve. Please call us at 801-808-4182 or email to arrange your first visit. Parents or guardians must be present for students under 18.

Still Have Questions?

Reach out directly — Sensei Chambers is happy to answer any questions and help you find the right program.
